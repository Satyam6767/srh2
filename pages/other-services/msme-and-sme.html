MSME and SME Loans

Micro, small, and medium enterprises (MSMEs) and small and medium enterprises (SMEs) are the backbone for the future growth of the Indian economy. These businesses provide employment to millions of people and aids in the industrialization of the rural and backward regions. MSMEs and SMEs play an important role as these make a significant contribution to domestic production and help to increase exports.

Investment in Plant, Machinery, and Equipment Annual Turnover

Medium Enterprises Up to INR 50 crore Up to INR 250 crore

Lack of structured finance and dearth of funds for the MSMEs and SMEs across the country is well-known. However, a recent report by IFC estimates the funding gap to 9.23% of the total GDP and only 16% of businesses are funded by the formal institutional channels.

SMEs and MSMEs have unique issues as by nature these are small enterprises and face several challenges. Most business owners are not highly educated and lack of financial literacy, which impedes access to formal lending. Another challenge faced by small enterprises is the unavailability of collateral, which further makes it difficult to access institutional credit.

What are the eligibility criteria?

Although the eligibility norms may vary from one institution to another, some of the common criteria include:

  • Available to Indian nationals including proprietors, partnership firms, private limited, and public limited companies
  • Manufacturing and service business
  • Profitable venture for at least two years or longer
  • Business plan showing future growth and expansion plans
  • Government registration as per the regulatory guidelines
  • Stable credit history and CIBIL score

What are the documents needed for these loans?

Most lenders do not require too much documentation when you apply for a loan. Some common documents needed include:

  • Proof of business ownership
  • Bank statements and account details
  • Three years financial statements including profit and loss account and balance sheet
  • KYC documents, which include PAN card and Aadhar card
  • GST registration
  • Incorporation certificate
  • Partnership deed, if applicable
  • UDYAM registration, if applicable
  • Existing loan details, if any
  • Industry-specific licenses, if any
  • Personal guarantee from partners, promoters, and directors (as applicable)
